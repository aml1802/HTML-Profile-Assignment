The Life and Career of Dwayne "The Rock" Johnson

Dwayne Douglas Johnson, known professionally as "The Rock," is an American actor, producer, and former professional wrestler born on May 2, 1972. A University of Miami alumnus, he is one of the highest-paid actors in the world and is known for his charismatic personality and incredible work ethic. Uses the WWE ring name “The Rock.”

Early Life and University of Miami

Dwayne Johnson was born in Hayward, California, to Rocky Johnson, a professional wrestler, and Ata Johnson. He attended the University of Miami on a full scholarship, where he played defensive tackle for the Miami Hurricanes football team. During his time at UM, he was part of the 1991 national championship team and graduated in 1995 with a Bachelor of General Studies degree in criminology and physiology.

From Wrestling to Hollywood

After a brief stint in the Canadian Football League, Johnson followed in his father's footsteps and became a professional wrestler in 1996. He quickly rose to fame in WWE as "The Rock," becoming one of the most popular wrestlers of all time. In 2001, he began transitioning to acting, eventually becoming one of Hollywood's biggest action stars with films like the Fast & Furious franchise, Jumanji, and Moana.

Major Achievements

  2. Highest-paid actor in the world (2016–2019)
